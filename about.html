About

Hi, I’m Niall Byrnes. I’m currently pursuing a PhD in physics at Imperial College London under the supervision of Dr. Matthew Foreman. My research is primarily focused on the scattering of polarised light in complex, mesoscopic scattering media. In particular, I have been exploring the application of random matrices to modelling the vectorial scattering matrix and have developed techniques for numerical simulations. For more information, feel free to check out some of my work.

Outside of work, I have an amateur interest in languages and language learning. Having lived in China for thee years, I have become a proficient user of standard Mandarin Chinese. I’m currently learning Italian and I’m keen to explore languages from other language families in the future. I also enjoy walking and hiking, both around cities and in nature, and can sometimes be found in a bouldering gym.

I worked as a mathematics teacher in an international high school in Shanghai for three years. At present, I occasionally do private tutoring in mathematics, physics and computer programming. I have also helped people with English proofreading and Chinese to English translations. If you are interested in any of these, please don’t hesitate to get in touch.
